About Us - NerdNet

Explore our Courses

The content that we display on NerdNet consists of a series of Computer Science courses: CSE224 (Basic C & Bash), CSE222 (Data Structures & Algorithms), and CSE223 (Data Structures & OOP in Java). Alongside, there is Computer Engineering courses, ENGR270 (Assembly and Microprocessors).

Empowering Future Programmers

Our goal is for anyone, regardless of their experience in programming, to learn programming in C and Java at an advanced level. Additionally, we aim to provide knowledge about bash, digital logic, such as binary systems and state machines, as well as assembly. We also cover discrete structures, often referred to as discrete math. Learning these topics will help strengthen your programming skills and broaden your perspective from low-level code to more advanced languages and their interaction with machines.

Where It All Began

We ourselves have taken these full courses and acquired all of our programming skills from them. These courses are video lectures provided by Nicholas Macias, Ph.D., and are also available on his YouTube channel, MyGeekAdventures. Although these videos are designed for college courses, he posts them for free online, allowing everyone to enjoy and learn from them.

Why We Started

NerdNet is made up of a one-man team. We started this website as a college assignment that encouraged us to step out of our comfort zone beyond what we learn in class, to go out on our own and create our own project, called a Service Learning Project (SLP). This project aimed to encourage us to learn new aspects of programming and create something new and unique to us. We decided to create a website that streams educational videos on Computer Science and Engineering. We began with no prior knowledge of how to build a website. Starting a project like this required us to learn HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Firebase, and API integration and understand how these elements tie in together to create NerdNet.
